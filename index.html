About Me

Hello! My name is Joshua Carter and I am from a small town called Ashland, Massachusetts. Growing up I was always curious about how much I could really do with the power of my home desktop, which inspired me to learn how to build PCs from scratch AND learn basic code and command prompts along the way. The hardware aspect of computing always fascinated me, while configuration of the hardware via Bios and OS always seemed like a hurdle to me. This difficulty I had with configuration during my first couple of builds eventually turned into a task where I launched headfirst into figuring out everything I could do with my BIOS, learning all I could do with the Windows/Linux OS in addition to its command prompts, setting up secure VPNs, and ultimately learning HTML and additional webpage makeup. I learned that I could easily be self taught in many areas of computing but would always struggle with fully learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript on my own. After working in the human services field for a few years out of high school with no college background, I decided it was finally time to turn my familiarities into comprehensible knowledge. This page marks my first full dive into creating and designing my first webpage entirely from scratch through the UPenn Coding Bootcamp program!
